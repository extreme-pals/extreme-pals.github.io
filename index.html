About Us

InfPals

InfPals is a Peer Learning and Support Program, aiming to bring together Year 1 students and helping them integrate into the Informatics world.

ExtremePals

Want to enter an official hackathon but feeling daunted by its unfamiliarity? Well, ExtremePals is for you!
This hackathon will run over one whole day for 12 hours with breaks for a workshop and lunch. Participants will be able to form small teams of up to 3-4 people to create a game together based on a provided theme and framework. We will award prizes for the best submissions.
Extreme PALS has the support of Volker Seeker, the lecturer for object oriented programming, a compulsory course for first year computer science students. By integrating the material with his course syllabus, you can level-up your Java skills with some hands-on experience, and don't worry if you're unfamiliar with Java! With a dedicated team of InfPals leaders around, you'll never be short of guidance (also guides are provided).
See this as an introduction to your hackathon journey.

Objective

The primary goal of Extreme PALS is to introduce students with limited programming experience to programming events in a friendly way. As students in the school of informatics, we see how some new students joining informatics come with a lot of prior experience, while others do not. Those who have not done much (if any) coding in the past are often too afraid to take part in any of the vast number of events happening around the year. These events such as hackathons, game jams, coding competitions are often organized by CompSoc and GameDevSoc, yet in PALS we noticed that none of them are aimed specifically at beginners.
Extreme PALS wants to fill this gap, bolstering the participantsâ€™ confidence in their abilities, encouraging them to engage with events outside of the academic curriculum and inspiring them to develop their own ideas. Participating 1st year students can also use this as fun preparation for their upcoming exams. As InfPALS leaders, we hope that this event will give the scheme lots of exposure and make a positive impression, which may contribute to the growth of the InfPALS scheme in the coming academic year.
